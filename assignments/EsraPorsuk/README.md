Author: Esra PORSUK
Course: Biomolecular Modeling  

Project Overview

In this assignment, the three-dimensional structure of Human Carbonic Anhydrase II was predicted using AlphaFold Server. The selected protein was obtained from the Week 4 protein list, and its FASTA sequence was used as the input for structure prediction.

Selected Protein

- Protein name: Human Carbonic Anhydrase II
- PDB ID: 1CA2
- Organism: Homo sapiens
- Protein chain: Chain A
- Method used for original experimental structure: X-ray diffraction
- AlphaFold input type: Protein FASTA sequence

Biological Function

Carbonic Anhydrase II is a zinc-dependent enzyme that catalyzes the reversible conversion of carbon dioxide and water into bicarbonate and protons. This reaction is important for pH regulation, carbon dioxide transport, and acid-base balance in biological systems.

Methodology

The FASTA sequence of Human Carbonic Anhydrase II was downloaded from the RCSB PDB database. The sequence was submitted to AlphaFold Server as a single protein chain with one copy. AlphaFold generated five predicted structural models. The first predicted model was selected for submission.

AlphaFold Prediction Result

The AlphaFold Server produced a highly confident predicted structure. The model was mostly colored blue, indicating high-confidence regions. The predicted TM-score value was 0.97, suggesting a reliable overall fold prediction.

Repository Contents

- `fold_1ca2_model_0.cif`: Predicted 3D structure of Human Carbonic Anhydrase II generated by AlphaFold.
- `fold_1ca2_summary_confidences_0.json`: Confidence score file generated by AlphaFold.
- `1ca2_pymol_render.png`: PyMOL visualization of the predicted Human Carbonic Anhydrase II structure.
- `README.md`: Explanation of the selected protein, methodology, and prediction result.

Conclusion

Human Carbonic Anhydrase II was successfully modeled using AlphaFold Server. The predicted structure showed high confidence, suggesting that the selected FASTA sequence was suitable for protein folding prediction.
