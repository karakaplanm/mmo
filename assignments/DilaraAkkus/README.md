# What is 1FHA?

1FHA is the three-dimensional structure of **human H-chain ferritin**, one of the major proteins responsible for iron storage in living organisms.

Ferritin acts as a biological "iron storage container." It protects cells from the toxic effects of free iron while keeping iron available for essential biological processes such as:

* Oxygen transport
* DNA synthesis
* Cellular respiration
* Energy production
* Redox metabolism

The structure deposited as 1FHA was historically important because it provided one of the first detailed views of the **human H-ferritin subunit** and revealed the location of its ferroxidase center, which is responsible for rapid iron oxidation and storage.

---

# Biological Function

Ferritin stores iron in a safe and soluble form.

The protein performs three major tasks:

### 1. Iron Capture

Free iron enters the ferritin protein through channels in the protein shell.

### 2. Iron Oxidation

Ferrous iron (Fe²⁺) is oxidized into ferric iron (Fe³⁺) at a specialized catalytic site known as the **ferroxidase center**.

Reaction:

Fe²⁺ → Fe³⁺

This oxidation prevents iron from generating harmful reactive oxygen species.

### 3. Iron Storage

The oxidized iron is deposited inside a hollow cavity within the ferritin shell, where thousands of iron atoms can be stored safely.

A complete ferritin particle can store approximately **4,000–4,500 iron atoms**.

---

# Why is H Ferritin Important?

Ferritin exists in two major subunit types:

## H Chain (Heavy Chain)

* Rapid iron uptake
* Strong ferroxidase activity
* Important for active iron metabolism
* Protects cells from oxidative stress

## L Chain (Light Chain)

* Better suited for long-term iron storage
* Promotes mineralization of the iron core

The H chain found in 1FHA is particularly important because it contains the catalytic site responsible for converting Fe²⁺ into Fe³⁺. The original study proposed that this ferroxidase site explains why H-rich ferritins absorb iron more rapidly than L-rich ferritins.

# Structural Features

## Protein Length

* 183 amino acids
* Single ferritin H-chain subunit

## Secondary Structure

The protein is composed primarily of:

* Alpha helices
* Four-helix bundle architecture

This helical arrangement forms a compact and highly stable fold.

## Quaternary Structure

Ferritin normally assembles into a:

* 24-subunit spherical shell
* Hollow nanocage structure
* Approximately 12 nm in diameter

The shell contains channels that allow iron ions to move in and out.

Similar ferritin assemblies have been observed in horse spleen apoferritin and other ferritin structures.

---

# Metal Binding Site

A major discovery from the 1FHA structure was the identification of a metal-binding region within the four-helix bundle.

This region:

* Binds iron ions
* Catalyzes iron oxidation
* Forms the ferroxidase center

The deposited structure contains an Fe(III) ion associated with the protein.

# Medical Relevance

Ferritin is essential for maintaining iron homeostasis.

Abnormal ferritin function is associated with:

* Iron overload disorders
* Neurodegenerative diseases
* Anemia
* Inflammatory diseases
* Cancer-related iron dysregulation

Because iron metabolism is critical to human health, ferritin has become an important target in biomedical research.

---

# Nanotechnology Applications

Ferritin is also widely used in biotechnology and nanotechnology because of its cage-like structure.

Applications include:

* Drug delivery systems
* Nanoparticle synthesis
* MRI contrast agent development
* Vaccine design
* Targeted cancer therapeutics

The hollow ferritin shell can be engineered to encapsulate metals, drugs, or imaging agents.

---

# Key Scientific Discovery of 1FHA

The 1991 Nature publication describing 1FHA demonstrated that:

1. Human H ferritin possesses a unique ferroxidase center.
2. This catalytic center explains rapid iron uptake.
3. The structure clarified functional differences between H-chain and L-chain ferritins.
4. Genetic engineering was used to improve crystal formation and solve the structure successfully.

---

# Summary

1FHA represents the crystal structure of human H-chain ferritin, a protein responsible for iron detoxification and storage. The structure revealed the ferroxidase center that converts Fe²⁺ to Fe³⁺, enabling safe iron storage within the ferritin nanocage. This discovery significantly advanced understanding of iron metabolism and remains an important reference structure in structural biology, biochemistry, medicine, and nanotechnology.
