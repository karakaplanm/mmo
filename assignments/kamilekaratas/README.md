## Project Overview
In this project, the 3D structure of Cerberus (CER1), a multivalent signaling antagonist critical for embryonic head development, was modeled *ab initio*. CER1 is structurally fascinating as it simultaneously binds and neutralizes three completely different signaling molecules: Wnt, Nodal, and BMP. 

## Methodology
The structural modeling was performed using **Google DeepMind AlphaFold 3**. 
* The target sequence (UniProt: O95816) was submitted as a monomer.
* The structural validation and confidence metrics are provided in the `summary_confidences_0.json` file.
* **Score Analysis:** The algorithm reported a high `"fraction_disordered": 0.64`. This is highly consistent with CER1's biological function; as a multivalent antagonist, it requires extensive intrinsically disordered regions (flexible loops) to adapt to and interact with multiple diverse target receptors.

## Structural Analysis and Visualization
The predicted model (`.cif`) was imported into **PyMOL** and colored based on per-residue confidence (pLDDT) metrics to separate the stable core from the flexible target-binding arms.

**Highlighted Structural Motif (The Cysteine-Knot):** While 64% of the protein acts as flexible binding arms, the structural integrity of the protein core is maintained by a highly conserved **Cysteine-knot**. In the provided PyMOL render (`cer1_pymol_render.png`), the cysteine residues forming these critical disulfide bridges are specifically highlighted as magenta sticks to demonstrate the rigid core topology of this inhibitor.
