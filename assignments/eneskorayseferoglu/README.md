## Project Overview
In this project, the 3D structure of the Sonic Hedgehog (SHH) pre-protein (462 amino acids), which plays a critical role in embryonic development, tissue differentiation, and organogenesis, was modeled *ab initio*. SHH is a unique target for structural biology due to its autocatalytic cleavage ability and subsequent cholesterol modification in cellular signaling pathways.

## Methodology
The structural modeling was performed using **Google DeepMind AlphaFold 3**. 
* The target sequence (UniProt: Q15465) was submitted as a monomeric structure.
* Among the 5 different conformations generated by the algorithm, `model_0` was selected as the reference structure due to its highest global confidence ranking.
* Structural validation and confidence metrics (including PTM and disorder fraction) are provided in the `summary_confidences_0.json` file.

## Structural Analysis and Visualization
The predicted `.cif` model was imported into **PyMOL** and colored according to the per-residue confidence score (pLDDT) metrics to evaluate regional stability:
* **Blue (pLDDT > 90):** High accuracy expected (Structured core domains).
* **Cyan/Yellow (90 > pLDDT > 50):** Low confidence (Flexible linker regions).
* **Orange (pLDDT < 50):** Intrinsically disordered tail regions (consistent with the 24% disorder fraction reported in the confidence summary).

**Highlighted Active Center:** In the high-resolution PyMOL render (`shh_pymol_render.png`), the autocatalytic active center residues, **Gly197 and Cys198**, where SHH cleaves itself prior to the cholesterol addition, are specifically highlighted as red spheres.

## Repository Contents
