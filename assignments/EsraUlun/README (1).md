## 🔬 Overview  
This file contains the three-dimensional structure of the Bovine Trypsin protein obtained from the Protein Data Bank (PDB) under the ID **2PTN**. Trypsin is one of the most well-known serine proteases and plays a crucial role in protein digestion by hydrolyzing peptide bonds at the carboxyl side of lysine and arginine residues.

---

## 🧬 What is Trypsin?  

- **Protein Name:** Trypsin  
- **PDB ID:** 2PTN  
- **Organism:** *Bos taurus* (Bovine)  
- **Protein Type:** Serine Protease  

### Main Functions  
- Catalyzes the digestion of proteins in the small intestine.  
- Cleaves peptide bonds specifically after lysine and arginine amino acids.  
- Participates in activation of other digestive enzymes such as chymotrypsinogen and procarboxypeptidases.  

### Catalytic Mechanism  
Trypsin belongs to the serine protease family and contains the classical catalytic triad:  

- **Serine (Ser195)**  
- **Histidine (His57)**  
- **Aspartate (Asp102)**  

These residues work together to hydrolyze peptide bonds efficiently.

## 🛠 Methodology  

1. The protein structure was searched on the Protein Data Bank (PDB) website using the keyword “Trypsin”.  
2. The crystal structure with the PDB ID **2PTN** was selected for analysis.  
3. The structure file was downloaded in `.pdb` format and visualized using molecular visualization software such as PyMOL.  
4. Structural regions, active site residues, and secondary structures were examined.  

---

## 📊 Structural Analysis and Visualization  

The 2PTN structure mainly consists of:  

- β-sheet–rich domains characteristic of serine proteases.  
- A well-defined catalytic pocket containing the catalytic triad.  
- Stabilizing disulfide bonds contributing to structural stability.  

### Important Structural Features  

- Compact globular enzyme structure.  
- Highly conserved active site geometry.  
- Presence of substrate-binding specificity pocket responsible for lysine/arginine selectivity.
